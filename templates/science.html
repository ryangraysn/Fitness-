What is 1RM and how this table is calculated

The one-repetition maximum (1RM) is an estimate of the maximum load a person can lift for one complete repetition. This table uses a common estimation formula: 1RM = Weight × (1 + Reps / 30). For example, 10 reps with 150 lb estimates 1RM ≈ 150 × (1 + 10/30) = 200 lb.

Use these estimates for programming and tracking progress. While they may not strictly predict real values, they are useful for comparing the difficulty of sets at different weights. Always prioritize technique and safety when testing maximal loads.

Tonnage

Tonnage is the total work performed, calculated on this site as the sum of (reps × weight) across sets for a session or day. Our graphs display one tonnage total for each day, however one rep max and relative intensity are only based on the most difficult set on a given day.

Wilks Score — formula & utility

The Wilks score is a way to compare strength (total weight lifted) across lifters of different bodyweights by applying a coefficient calculated from the lifter's bodyweight. Each sex uses a set of coefficients in the polynomial denominator; the score is typically computed as:

Wilks score = (weight_lifted) × (constant / polynomial(bodyweight))

In the table above we use a constant of 600 and the sex-specific polynomial coefficients embedded in the app (the same coefficients used elsewhere in this codebase). Higher Wilks scores indicate more strength relative to bodyweight.

Utility: use Wilks to compare performances between lifters of different sizes, to track relative strength improvements, or to normalise progress when bodyweight changes. Remember Wilks is an estimate — competition federations may use updated scoring formulas (e.g., IPF points). Treat Wilks as a comparison tool, not an absolute measure of ability.
